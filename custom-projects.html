Best-fit custom requests

Strong custom requests are specific enough to review without oversharing.

  • Commercial concept or campaign need.
  • Property, event, outdoor, or location-based story.
  • Desired photo, video, or mixed deliverables.
  • General timing and use case.

Creative planning

Use the planning stage to match the idea with portfolio style.

  • Reference visuals and mood.
  • Story beats and must-have shots.
  • Audience and distribution channel.
  • Constraints that should be reviewed first.

Scope boundaries

Custom does not mean automatic approval or feasibility.

  • No public confirmation of operating conditions.
  • No promise of creator availability.
  • No private location or customer details in first contact.
  • No production guarantee from this static site.

Next-step output

The first useful output is a clearer brief.

  • Project summary.
  • Open questions.
  • Portfolio styles to compare.
  • Details needed before any scope conversation.

Start carefully

Describe the creative goal first.

Custom requests should begin with goals, not assumptions about logistics or approvals.
